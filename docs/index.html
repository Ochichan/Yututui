● The good stuff

Everything, one key away

It's all keyboard, all fast, and all yours to rebind. Here's what you get the moment you type ytt.

Ctrl R

DJ Gem streaming that reads the room

Press Ctrl+R and ytm-tui starts streaming built around what you're hearing. A Gemini-powered Queue Director lines up songs that fit the mood — Focused, Balanced, or Discovery. Press w for the plain-language reasoning.

album art v

Album art, lyrics — and the video

Actual cover images drawn right in the Player, with time-synced lyrics scrolling underneath. And when hearing isn't enough: press v and the music video floats over your terminal in its own little mpv window.

Tab ▾

Six catalogs, one search box

YouTube Music is home base, but the search box also speaks SoundCloud, Audius, Jamendo, Internet Archive, and Radio Browser. Flip sources with Tab, or search them all at once — every result tagged [SRC].

scrobble

Every listen, on the record

Last.fm and ListenBrainz, done properly: Now Playing updates, the half-track/4-minute rule, likes synced as loves. Offline listens queue on disk and deliver themselves when you're back — even from the headless daemon.

ytt transfer

Move in from Spotify

Liked songs and playlists migrate to YouTube Music with one command — checkpointed, resumable, CJK-safe matching, and a report for anything it wasn't sure about. Works the other way too, plus JSON/CSV backups of everything.

n d

Playlists and downloads, kept local

Build playlists right in the Library (DJ Gem can build them for you), drop Spotify imports into them, and press d to save any track as an m4a with cover art and tags — played offline from the Downloads tab.

⏯ ⏭ ⏮

Control it from everywhere

Media keys, macOS Control Center, a menu-bar / tray mini player, Linux MPRIS widgets, ytt -r from any terminal — or no terminal at all with the headless daemon. The music doesn't care where the order came from.

,

Unmistakably yours

13 built-in themes (Gruvbox, Nord, Dracula, Tokyo Night, Rosé Pine…), all 34 color roles editable in hex, a 10-band EQ with presets from Flat to Jazz, animations from a calm still screen to a spinning ASCII donut — and a retro mode that runs in a bare Linux console, ASCII album art included.

?

The app remembers the keys

Forget a shortcut? Press ? for the full, always-current cheat sheet — in your language, with your custom bindings. Korean jamo work as-is (ㅂ = q), and everything on screen is clickable if the mouse is more your speed.

● Streaming with its reasons

It doesn't just autoplay. It can tell you why.

Most players guess your next song and hope. ytm-tui's streaming plans a set — a core, a bridge, a discovery — then keeps the music flowing as your queue runs dry. Press w and it shows its work, in plain language, no math.

● Leaving Spotify?

Your library moves in with one command.

Liked songs, playlists, the lot — matched track by track (NFKC-normalized and CJK-safe, so K-pop and J-pop metadata survive), written to YouTube Music or into local playlists, with likes kept in order. Every job is checkpointed: rate limit, Ctrl-C, power cut — resume picks up where it left off. Nothing ambiguous is silently guessed; it lands in a report you can review.

Prefer to stay in the app? Settings → Accounts → Import from Spotify… picks a playlist and imports it in the background while the music keeps playing. Exports and Exportify-style CSV/JSON backups ride the same engine.

ytt transfer
$ ytt auth spotify --client-id <your-app> # one time, PKCE — no secret
$ ytt transfer import liked --to likes

$ ytt transfer backup --dir ~/music-backup --csv # every YTM playlist → JSON + CSV

● Last.fm · ListenBrainz

Your listening history, wherever you keep it.

ytm-tui scrobbles like a good citizen: Now Playing as the song starts, the scrobble itself at the classic half-track-or-4-minutes mark, and your in-app likes synced to Last.fm as loves. Connect from Settings → Accounts, or headless with ytt auth lastfm.

Listens are written to a crash-safe queue on disk before any network attempt — so a flight, a dead Wi-Fi, or a kill -9 loses nothing. They deliver themselves when you're back online, from the TUI or the daemon alike. Self-hosted ListenBrainz? Point api_url at it.

● Remote control · daemon · OS integration

The player is wherever you are

Once ytt is playing — or the headless daemon is, no terminal required — boss it around from anywhere: another shell, your media keys, Control Center, the tray. It does the one thing you asked, prints a tidy line, and gets out of the way.

ytt -r ppPlay / pause
ytt -r nextSkip to the next song
ytt -r volume 40Set the volume outright
ytt -r seek-to 90Jump to a timestamp
ytt -r streaming onSwitch endless streaming on
ytt -r status --jsonNow-playing snapshot, script-ready
ytt daemon start --resumeHeadless: restore the saved queue and play
ytt -r play "lofi"Daemon: search and play the first hit
ytt daemon stopStop the daemon and reap mpv
ytt -r status →
[playing] クリスマスソング — back number • vol 80% • 1/4 • streaming on
# wire it to your media keys (i3 / sway)
bindsym XF86AudioPlay exec ytt -r pp
bindsym XF86AudioNext exec ytt -r next

Launching ytt twice won't start a second player fighting over your speakers — it just hands you the controls. The daemon restores queue order, cursor, shuffle/repeat, and streaming state, and keeps scrobbling while it's at it.

Control Center macOS

The real Now Playing card: artwork, a working scrubber, next/previous, even a Like button. Your AirPods' stem click does what it should. And the same mini player rides your menu bar — ytt-tray ships with the macOS build.

▦ SMTC + tray mini player Windows

Media overlay with artwork and seek, plus an optional tray companion: left-click for a mini player (Now / Queue / Stream / Tune), right-click for the full menu. ytt-tray --install-startup if you want it at login.

◆ MPRIS Linux

A first-class org.mpris.MediaPlayer2 citizen: playerctl, GNOME and KDE widgets, waybar — they all just see a music player, because it is one.

✓ No orphaned mpv. Ever.

Kill it, crash it, close the window, log off — signal hooks, a panic hook, a Windows Job Object, and a PID lifeline file make sure the audio dies with the app.

✓ Instant skips

The next track's stream URL is resolved while the current one plays, so "next" starts in a blink, not in three seconds. Gapless by default.

✓ Crash-safe by paranoia

Scrobbles hit disk before the network. Transfers checkpoint every few tracks. Config saves are atomic. Your data assumes the worst so you don't have to.

✓ ytt doctor

One command checks mpv, yt-dlp, ffmpeg and your folders, then tells you exactly what to fix — in plain words, not a stack trace.

● Two minutes to music

Pick your platform. Paste one line.

Each command installs ytt and its helpers (mpv, yt-dlp, ffmpeg) for you. Then type three letters and you're listening.

ytt@macos ~
$ brew install Ochichan/tap/ytm-tui

Homebrew pulls in mpv, yt-dlp and ffmpeg automatically — and installs ytt-tray, the menu-bar companion, alongside. Don't have Homebrew? Grab it at brew.sh first.

> scoop bucket add extras
> scoop bucket add ytm-tui https://github.com/Ochichan/scoop-bucket
> scoop install ytm-tui

Uses Scoop (scoop.sh). Installs ytt plus mpv, yt-dlp, ffmpeg — and the YtmTui Tray mini player, which stays out of your way until you want it.

# Nix — the most reproducible option (helpers included)
$ nix run github:Ochichan/ytm-tui
# Arch (AUR)
$ yay -S ytm-tui-bin
# Any other distro — prebuilt binary
$ curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Ochichan/ytm-tui/main/install.sh | bash

The curl one-liner verifies the checksum and adds ytt to your PATH. It installs only ytt — run ytt doctor afterwards to check the helpers.

# macOS / Linux — needs Rust (rustup.rs)
$ ./install.sh --build
# Windows (PowerShell)
> powershell -ExecutionPolicy Bypass -File .\install.ps1 --build

Clone the repo first. Building takes a few minutes — a great excuse to make coffee.

Then run it: $ ytt …and press ? at any time for every key. The app teaches you the rest.

Something off? Run ytt doctor — it checks mpv, yt-dlp and ffmpeg and tells you exactly what to fix. No YouTube account needed; public tracks play anonymously, and your own account is one cookies.txt away if you want it.
