● The good stuff

Everything, one key away

It's all keyboard, all fast, and all yours to rebind. Here's what you get the moment you type ytt.

Ctrl R

DJ Gem streaming that reads the room

Press Ctrl+R and ytm-tui starts streaming built around what you're hearing. A Gemini-powered Queue Director lines up songs that fit the mood — Focused, Balanced, or Discovery. Press w for the plain-language reasoning.

album art

Real album art. In a terminal.

Yes — actual cover images, drawn right in the Player on terminals that can show them. Pixels where you least expect them. Synced lyrics scroll underneath, karaoke for one.

ytt -r

Drive it from anywhere

Wire your media keys once — ⏯ ⏭ ⏮ — and never alt-tab back. ytt -r next, ytt -r pp, ytt -r status. A stray second launch just hands you the controls.

s

Search, library, queue, lyrics

s to search, Enter to play. l for your library, c for the queue, Shift+L for time-synced lyrics. Favorites, history and downloads, all in one list.

,

Unmistakably yours

11 built-in themes (Gruvbox, Nord, Dracula, Tokyo Night, Rosé Pine…), every color tweakable in hex, every key rebindable, a 10-band EQ, and animations from a calm still screen to a spinning ASCII donut.

?

The app remembers the keys

Forget a shortcut? Press ? for the full, always-current cheat sheet, grouped by screen — in your language, with your custom bindings. Even Korean jamo work: ㅂ = q.

● Streaming with its reasons

It doesn't just autoplay. It can tell you why.

Most players guess your next song and hope. ytm-tui's streaming plans a set — an anchor, a bridge, a lift, a wind-down — then keeps the music flowing as your queue runs dry. Press w and it shows its work, in plain language, no math.

● Two minutes to music

Pick your platform. Paste one line.

Each command installs ytt and its helpers (mpv, yt-dlp, ffmpeg) for you. Then type three letters and you're listening.

ytt@macos ~
$ brew install Ochichan/tap/ytm-tui

Homebrew pulls in mpv, yt-dlp and ffmpeg automatically. Don't have Homebrew? Grab it at brew.sh first.

> scoop bucket add extras
> scoop bucket add ytm-tui https://github.com/Ochichan/scoop-bucket
> scoop install ytm-tui

Uses Scoop (scoop.sh). Installs ytt plus mpv, yt-dlp, ffmpeg, and a YtmTui Tray shortcut.

# Nix — the most reproducible option
$ nix run github:Ochichan/ytm-tui
# Arch (AUR)
$ yay -S ytm-tui-bin
# Any other distro — prebuilt binary
$ curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Ochichan/ytm-tui/main/install.sh | bash

The curl one-liner verifies the checksum and adds ytt to your PATH. It installs only ytt — run ytt doctor afterwards to check the helpers.

# macOS / Linux — needs Rust (rustup.rs)
$ ./install.sh --build
# Windows (PowerShell)
> powershell -ExecutionPolicy Bypass -File .\install.ps1 --build

Clone the repo first. Building takes a few minutes — a great excuse to make coffee.

Then run it: $ ytt …and press ? at any time for every key. The app teaches you the rest.

Something off? Run ytt doctor — it checks mpv, yt-dlp and ffmpeg and tells you exactly what to fix.

● Remote control

Reach it without leaving your work

Once ytt is playing, boss it around from another terminal — or straight from your keyboard's media keys. It does the one thing you asked, prints a tidy line, and gets out of the way. The music screen doesn't even flicker.

ytt -r ppPlay / pause
ytt -r nextSkip to the next song
ytt daemon start --resumeStart the headless music daemon
ytt -r play "lofi"Daemon: search and play
ytt -r enqueue "city pop"Daemon: search and queue
ytt -r streaming onSwitch endless streaming on
ytt -r statusWhat's playing right now?
ytt daemon stopStop the daemon and reap mpv
ytt -r status →
[playing] クリスマスソング — back number • vol 80% • 1/4 • streaming on
# wire it to your media keys (i3 / sway)
bindsym XF86AudioPlay exec ytt -r pp
bindsym XF86AudioNext exec ytt -r next

Launching ytt twice won't start a second player. For terminal-free playback, the daemon restores your saved queue order, cursor, shuffle/repeat, and streaming state.
